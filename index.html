PIPING DIVISION

Piping Division is AN ENGINEERING division , PART OF EPC Company TO DESIGN AND ENGINEER PLANT PIPING SYSTEM ACCORDING TO THE INTERNATIONAL DESIGN CODES, COMPANY SPECIFICATIONS & ENGINEERING PRACTICES.

Piping SYSTEM is ACTING IN THE PLANT SIMILAR AS network of veins and arteries in our body. It transports fluids from one place to another, from one equipment to another.

PLANT PIPING ENGINEERING IS A CRITICAL SCOPE , IT NEEDS SUFFECIENT knowledge and experience to BE PERFORMED successfully.

Pipes

Definition
A pipe is a piping element used to transfer fluid between equipments and other piping elements (valves, fittings,…) to a achieve process.

Types of Pipe

Pipes

Materials

-NPS is equivalent to OD for pipe size is 14” and larger

Wall Thickness = (OD-ID) /2

Fittings

Definition
A fitting is a piping element that connects a pipe in order to facilitate the flow direction.

-Pipe fittings are used for one or more functions:
1. Change of direction. Elbows 90&45 and Bends)
2. Pipe branch. (Equal /Reducing Tee, Weldolet, Sockolet, Threadolet, …)
3. Reduction in pipe size. (Concentric Reducer, Eccentric Reducer and Swage)
4. Closing pipes or branches. (Caps and Plugs)
5. Adaptor.
6. Pipe joint. (Flange, Coupling and Union)

Fitting Ends
* Socket Weld End (SW)
* Plain End (PE)
* Bevel End (BE)
* Flanged End(FE)
* Threaded End(TE)

Plot Plan

- The Plot Plan is a Graphical arrangement of the intended equipment placement.
- The Plot plan is one of the key documents produced during the engineering phase in any processing facility.
- The Plot plan is the only source for locating the Coordinates of all Equipment and Plant features
- There are Three Types of Plot plans:

Overall Site Plan

- The Overall Site Plan identifies the Total Plant Area (Existing and/or New) in relation to the general surrounding.
- It defines the boundaries or perimeters within which the plant is to be constructed.

The Overall Site Plan should be indicate the following (but not limited to):

* All perimeter walls, fences and gates.
* New Plant boundary limits provided by the company.
* Location of existing units.
* Location of existing utilities.
* Location of existing building.
* Location of existing railroad.
* Location of existing major underground features .
* Location of existing flare stacks.

General Site Plan

- The General Plot Pan identifies the extent of the New Facilities including Storage, Loading, Unloading and Utility Facilities, Administration and other Buildings.
- The General Plot Plan indicates the Boundary Limits of the Project, including Plant North-South Orientation, True North / South, Prevailing Wind Direction, road coordinate, HPFS elevation …. etc.


The General Plot Plan should be indicate the following (but not limited to):

* All new roads and designated accesses outside unit battery limits
* Location of pipe racks and sleeper ways outside unit battery limits.
* Location of all Features outside the Limits of individual the Units Plot Plans, but within the limits of the General Plot Plan.

Unit Site Plan

- The Unit Plot Plan represents the detailed Layout of a specific Unit within the General Plot Plan, such as Equipment, Structures, Roads, Paving, etc.
- The Unit Plot Plan show coordinate location for all plant features, buildings, structures and equipment within the area limit of the unit

(The only recognized source of coordinate location)


The General Plot Plan should be indicate the following (but not limited to):

* Coordinates and location of all plant features and equipment.
* Limits of paved and unpaved areas.
* Top of dike/curb.
* Building.
* Deluge skids.
* Future areas.

Piping Isometrics

- Piping isometric drawing is a three dimensional representation of pipe with all information necessary to purchase , fabricate, install and test the line.
- The isometric drawing extracted from the 3D model of the plant design system (PDS, PDMS, etc.)
- Piping Isometric drawing shall provide Adequate information to fabricate shop pipe-work.


The Isometric Sheet should be indicate the following (but not limited to):

* Bill Of Material with description
* Line Data (Number, Size, Conditions ,insulation Type and thickness, Test Type).
* Tracing type (Steam / Electrical)
* North Arrow at the top left of the drawing
* Flow Direction, All inline / attached Instruments.
* Equipment Nozzle Number with coordinates and elevations.
* Coordinate of Line Continuation.
* All piping components & specialties (valves, elbow, strainer, trap, etc.).
* All pipe supports.
* Special line features (slope, reinforcing pads, weep holes…etc.).

Piping Isometrics

Line Data

The Line Data should be include:

1. Line Number :

- Line Size.
- Fluid (Commodity) Code.
- Line Serial Number.
- Piping Material Class.
- Insulation Type.


2. Line ID

- Unit/Area No.
- Fluid (Commodity) Code.
- Line Serial Number.


3. Line Condition

- Pressure (Operating/Design/Test).
- Temperature (Operating/Design).
- Test Type.
- Others.

Bill of Material

All material shall be listed in a tabulated Format located at the Top Right Hand Side of the Drawing and shall include the following:
- Component short Description.
- Size.
- Item Code.
- Quantity.
- Weight (if Required)
- Others.

Notes

- The Location of the Notes shall be directly below the Bill of Material List.
- The Notes on the drawings shall be kept to a minimum to highlight any consideration that clarifies a specific part on the drawing.

Piping Isometrics

North Arrow

The North Arrow shall be located at the Top (Left or Right)Corner of the drawing.


Stress Signatures

Stress signatures (By, Checked and Approval) shall be placed in the signature boxes located in the left corner of the drawing.


Legends

The Legend shall be directly the Below the drawing to describe drawing symbol.

- Piping General Arrangement drawing is a plan view represents the piping layout (Piping, Equipment, Structure, etc.)within certain area with respect to other plant facility in this area.

for example:

* Racks and Multi-Levels Structures Section and Elevation Views to be inside the same Drawing.
- The drawing extracted from the 3D model of the plant design system (PDS, PDMS, etc.)


The General Arrangement Drawing should be indicate the following (but not limited to):

* Area limits.
* All equipment outlines/coordinates/reference point.
* Line numbers/Flow direction.
* The platforms, ladders, structures and stairways.
* Supports codes, location and orientations.
* Drain funnels locations.

Equipment Orientation

Emergency Systems Survivability Assessment (ESSA)

- The Equipment Orientation Drawings shall be prepared for the Static Equipment (Tanks, Column / Towers, Pig Traps, Vertical and Horizontal Vessels).


Nozzle Orientation is not prepared for Heat Exchangers


- The Equipment Orientation Drawings shall be issued up to the “Design Issue”.
- After the Design Issue, all updates shall be made in the form of Mark-Up and comments on Supplier drawings.


The Equipment Orientation Drawing should be indicate the following (but not limited to):

* Equipment nozzles orientations and projections.
* Equipment platform details (orientation, elevations and dimensions).
* Equipment Drop-out areas (especially for towers and vertical vessel).
* Davit details (height, location).
* Skirt type or lugs (for vertical vessels and towers).
* Saddle type (fixed or sliding) and height (for horizontal vessel).
* Nozzle table (number, size, rating, etc.).

Tie In List

Tie-In Point is the point where the new piping connects to the existing pipe /equipment.

Information Needs for Tie-In

- P&ID Showing Tie-In schematic location proposed By Process Engineer .
- Type of shutdown.
- General Arrangement Drawing for existing area.
- Piping Material Classes for Existing line.
- Pipe Supports of the existing line.
- Site Visit.


Tie-In Considerations

- Safety
- Shutdown & Isolation philosophy provided by company .
- Fluid of existing line in addition to actual operating conditions.
- Conditions of existing line.
- Tie-In test type.
- Available space for Tie-In.
- Constructability ( considering technical capabilities of the construction contractor).


Tie-In Number

- Tie-In numbers for the same plants or units in one project shall have an identical Numbering System.
- If a Tie-In has been cancelled it shall be indicated in the Remark Column of the Tie-In list “Cancelled”
- The Tie-In numbers shall be indicated on the P&ID’s, Piping General Arrangement drawing and Isometrics and should be tagged as follow:

Tie In List

Tie-In Type

The Type of Tie-In connections shall be indicated in the proper column of the Tie-In list and shall be as follows:

HT Hot Tap.
DB Drilled Branch.
CF Cut pipe and adding Flange to pipe.
CP Cut pipe without adding flange.
BF Bolt to existing Flange.
SF Connect to existing Screwed Fitting.

Risk Reduction measures

Tie-In Connection Size

- The size of Tie-In connection defined in the Tie-In list is the size where the Tie-In shall be connected (not the line size indicated on the P&ID)
- For Hot Taps and Drilled Branches, the size shall be identified by the header size X branch size.

Tie-In End Preparation

- The Tie-In connection point between the New and Existing pipe shall have one of the following End Preparation:

Type Description
BW Butt Weld
SW Socket Weld
TC Treaded Connection
FF Flat Face
RF Raised Face
RJ Ring Joint

Tie In List

Tie-In Connection Rating/Sched.

- For Flange connections the rating of existing flange shall be added in the list and verified at site.
- For Weld connection either by cold cut or hot tap the schedule of existing lien shall be added and measured at site.

Tie-In Connection Testing

- NDE shall be the normal practice during the execution of the Tie-In unless otherwise required by the company.
- For threaded connection SF or bolting to existing flange BF the test shall be considered during the commissioning and start up activities as part of the service test to check leak only.

Shutdown Type

- The shut down type of the various Tie-In shall be scheduled and defined by the company assisted by Enppi Project team.
- Type shall be indicated in the Tie-In list and shall be as follow:

Type Description
N No shutdown required
L Lline shutdown
S Specific system shutdown ( to be detailed in remarks column)
P Partial shutdown
R Train shutdown
U Unit shutdown
T Total plant shutdown
